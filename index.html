Overview

Take control of a college football program and lead the team to victory! Old School Football Coach gives you the true college football coach experience. Set your strategy, pick your plays, and watch the action unfold in front of you. Get ready for full game simulations with complex AI behaviors, presented in a retro 2d style.

Want to build a team from the ground up? Old School Football Coach has a career mode that allows you to grow your team year-after-year on your quest to become the next college football dynasty. Set depth charts, recruit new players, and design new plays on your road to be the #1 team in the nation!

Features

  • Design your league with editable teams and conferences. Decide which team you want to coach.
  • Each player is unique with 15+ attributes that determine how good they are! Review your players and set your depth chart.
  • Choose if you want to control your players directly or take a more relaxed approach by only calling out the plays.
  • Design new plays to outsmart the competition.
  • Postseason bowls and playoffs are highly customizable! Configure the number of spots available as well as conference tie-ins.
  • Research high school players and compete against other schools as you attempt to recruit them to your team.
  • Browse stats and rankings from around the league.

Build 21-06 Available

Announcement
May 31, 2021 (Link)

Happy Memorial Day! A new development build (21-06) of Old School Football Coach is now publicly available, which, in addition to various bug fixes, includes 2 major features!

Player Fatigue

The football players on your team have always had a stamina attribute, but until now it didn’t have a major impact on their performance. That all changes with this update. Players will now get fatigued as they play, and as their condition worsens so will their other abilities. You can combat this by temporarily benching your tired players and give them time to rest and recover.

UI Scaling

Previously, playing the game on a small screen or high resolution display could make the UI elements small and hard to read. This build adds a UI scale adjustment to the Game Options. It is now possible to make the UI elements 1.5x or 2x bigger! This may seem like a small change, but behind the scenes a lot of code needed to be written to make this possible. Though, do note that the game doesn’t play well (and will warn you) when the UI is scaled with the window set at a low resolution. This effectively reduces the amount of space that UI elements have to fit inside of, which some screens and menus in the game can’t yet accommodate.
